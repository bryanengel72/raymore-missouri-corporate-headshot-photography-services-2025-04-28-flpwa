How to Prepare for Your Corporate Headshot Session

A professional headshot is more than just a photo—it's your first impression in the digital world. Whether you're updating your LinkedIn profile, company website, or marketing materials, being well-prepared for your session ensures the best possible results.

Choosing the Right Attire

Your clothing choice significantly impacts the success of your headshot. Opt for solid colors that complement your skin tone and avoid busy patterns or trendy styles that may quickly date your photo. Dark blues, grays, and neutral tones photograph exceptionally well and project professionalism. Ensure your clothes are pressed and fit properly—anything too loose or tight can be distracting.

Grooming and Preparation

Schedule any haircuts or color treatments 1-2 weeks before your session to allow for a natural settling period. For makeup, aim for a polished but natural look that enhances your features without being overly dramatic. Men should consider a fresh shave or neat trim for facial hair. Get plenty of rest the night before to ensure you look refreshed and energetic.

Posing and Expression

Practice your smile and facial expressions in the mirror before your session. A genuine smile that reaches your eyes creates the most engaging and approachable headshot. We'll guide you through various poses, but familiarity with your best angles can boost your confidence during the shoot. Remember to maintain good posture and keep your shoulders relaxed.

Scheduling Considerations

Book your session during a time when you won't feel rushed. Avoid scheduling important meetings immediately before or after your shoot. This allows you to remain relaxed and focused during the session. Consider morning appointments when you're typically fresh and energetic.

Day-of Preparation Tips

  • Bring a backup outfit or additional accessories for variety
  • Arrive 10-15 minutes early to settle in and review your goals
  • Bring any specific examples of styles you prefer
  • Stay hydrated and avoid heavy meals before the session
  • Bring touch-up supplies for quick adjustments

Communication is Key

Share your goals and preferences with your photographer before the session. Discuss any specific requirements for your industry or brand guidelines you need to follow. This ensures we capture images that align with your professional objectives while showcasing your authentic personality.

Quick Pre-Session Checklist

  • ✓ Select and prepare outfits
  • ✓ Schedule grooming appointments
  • ✓ Practice poses and expressions
  • ✓ Plan adequate time for the session
  • ✓ Prepare questions for your photographer
